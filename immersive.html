Immersive Virtual Reality In Learning

A tool to aid in learning and understanding of projection,section and intersection of solids
Project Guide:Prof.(Dr) Pradeep Yammiyawar
Final Year Design Project-Phase 1
Duration:Aug 2013-Nov 2013
Keywords:Learning,3D visualization,Architecture

About the Project

Architecture and design students, and students in certain technical fields need to have good spatial abilities. These students are expected to be good at imagining spaces and objects in 3D and manipulating them in their minds. To initiate students into 3D visualization, they are taught many related courses, depending on their domain. However, all the departments, be it architecture, design or an engineering fields, founds it’s advanced 3D graphics course on that of engineering drawing. In this course, which is usually introduced in the very semester of undergraduate studies, students are taught to deal with points, lines, solids, surfaces, etc. Since this is a sudden departure from their usual visualization techniques, many topics in this course are perceived to be difficult, by students who are to learn as well as instructors who are to teach the subject. In our project, we want to engage students in learning specific topics from the course of engineering drawing by giving them a platform which allows them to manipulate geometric solids. The platform is borrows from immersive virtual reality and gives students the opportunity to visualize and explore projections and intersections of solids in a dynamic way..


Objective

Through this project our aim is to develop a platform that aids in the instruction of engineering drawing for students of architecture and allied fields of design and engineering. Based in immersive virtual reality, the platform allows students to view common geometric objects and complicated solids in 3D. Additionally, they can manipulate these objects to view different projections, intersections, sections and surfaces. This would allow students a more hands-on and practical experience with 3D visualization and drawing, and would let them learn the subject constructively.
